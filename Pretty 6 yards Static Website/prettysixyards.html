CHANDERI SAREES

The weaving culture of Chanderi emerged between the 2nd and 7th centuries.
Chanderi saris are produced from three kinds of fabric: pure silk, Chanderi cotton and silk cotton.
The saris are among the finest in India and are known for their gold and silver brocade or zari.

MEKHALA SAREES

Ornamental designs on the mekhela-chadors are traditionally woven, never printed.
Sometimes a woven pattern, called the pari, is stitched along the sides of a chador, or along the bottom of a mekhela.
These are the best sarees when it comes to show the tradition.

BANDHANI SAREES

Bandhani is a type of tie-dye textile decorated by plucking the cloth with the fingernails into many tiny bindings that form a figurative design.
Produces variety of patterns like Chandrakala, Bavan Baug, Shikari.
The main colour used in Bandhana are yellow, red, blue, green and black.

CHIFFON SAREES

Chiffon to shimmer through, revealis a lightweight, balanced plain-woven sheer fabric, or gauze, like gossamer.
Chiffon is most commonly used in evening wear.
It is also a popular fabric used in blouses, ribbons, scarves and lingerie.
